Vaastu Shastra: Ancient Science of Harmony & Architecture

Vaastu Shastra is the traditional Indian system of architecture and design, focusing on aligning your living and working spaces with the natural forces and elements of the universe. Its principles are believed to bring peace, prosperity, and well-being to the inhabitants by harnessing positive energies and minimizing negative influences.
Discover the secrets of Vaastu and how you can apply them to your home and life!

Earth (Bhumi)
Symbolizes stability and support. The land on which a structure is built is crucial for its energy. Balanced earth element brings harmony, strength, and maturity.
Tip: Choose a regular-shaped plot for best results.
Water (Jal)
Represents purity, flow, and prosperity. Placement of water sources (tanks, wells) is key. Water in the right direction brings clarity and new opportunities.
Tip: Place water features in the North-East for abundance.
Fire (Agni)
Symbolizes energy, passion, and transformation. The kitchen and electricals should be in the South-East for optimal energy.
Tip: Avoid placing fire sources in the North-East.
Air (Vayu)
Denotes movement, freshness, and health. Proper ventilation and window placement ensure vibrant energy.
Tip: Ensure windows open to the North and East for fresh air.
Space (Akash)
Represents expansion and openness. Open spaces and clutter-free centers allow energy to flow.
Tip: Keep the center of your home open and light.

Vaastu Directions & Their Significance

Direction Ideal Use Element
North-East (Ishan) Pooja/Prayer Room, Water Source Water
South-East (Agneya) Kitchen, Electricals Fire
South-West (Nairutya) Master Bedroom, Heavy Storage Earth
North-West (Vayavya) Guest Room, Storage Air
Center (Brahmasthan) Open Space, Living Room Space

Quick Vaastu Tips for a Positive Home

  • Entrance: Main door should face East or North for positive energy.
  • Kitchen: Place in the South-East; avoid North-East.
  • Bedroom: Master bedroom in South-West; avoid North-East for bedrooms.
  • Living Room: Should be in the North or East part of the house.
  • Bathrooms: Place in West or North-West; avoid South-East and North-East.
  • Mirrors: Avoid mirrors facing the bed or main door.
  • Colors: Use light, earthy tones for walls; avoid too much black or red.
  • Plants: Keep Tulsi or money plant in the North or East; avoid cactus indoors.
  • Declutter: Keep the center of your home open and free of heavy objects.
